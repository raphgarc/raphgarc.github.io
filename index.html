Bounty Island

Previous Next

Bounty Island is a fun local multiplayer game (2 to 4 players) where the goal is to dig and bring back as many chests possible to your team's base. But beware, because you can also dig up bombs! You can also steal the other team's chests, hit them with your shovel to make them drop their chests, throw the bombs at them before they explode and push your enemies into the water.

Bounty Island was a game created for a game jam around the theme 'bounty'. With only three days at our disposal, we decided to create a multiplayer minigame loosely based on the mine sweeper game. Our main goal was for players to have fun and to find multiple game strategies: protecting your teammate while he digs a chest, following your opponents and stealing their chests at the last moment, targeting only difficult and rare chests...

    Easy Order

    Previous Next

    Easy Order is an online application that helps running an entire restaurant. Not for a brand of restaurant, or an online command service, but for one unique real restaurant. From the arrival of the client at the restaurant to him paying for his meal, everything can be managed on the site. The client will be assigned to a table among the available ones and make an order. It will then be sent to the kitchens; the waiter will be signaled when it is ready and will bring it to the table.

    Easy Order features 4 different kinds of accounts:

  • Clients: They can make an order, follow their progression, see the bill and specify the tip to get a final bill brought by the waiter
  • Waiters: They can see the tips they have received along the day, follow the progression of the orders they manage, get notified when they can bring one to the client
  • Cooks: They can signal they began the cooking of an order, and when they finished cooking it
  • Managers: They can observe the orders taken, how they progress, change the menu, assign a new client to a table and a waiter

    • Easy Order was a school project with the only directive being to create a website using PHP and databases. This was done as a duo with Gaspard Boutry, in twelve days (Dec. 2017). Programmed in HTML, CSS, PHP, JavaScript, Ajax, MySQL, Bootstrap. The website was programmed in french and english.

    Eternity

    Previous Next

    Eternity II is originally a puzzle board game composed of 256 pieces, each piece composed of 4 half-patterns that need to be next to the same half-pattern. Unlike most puzzle games representing an image or having one unique solution, Eternity has neither of them, proving itself to be a very hard puzzle with over 10 to the power 507 possible solutions. Launched in 2007, only very few people have managed to find one of the possible solutions. This program was not only an electronic version of the puzzle, but also offered an algorithm that could solve portions of the puzzle.

    This program offered two modes: playing the official Eternity II puzzle (256 pieces), and playing custom smaller boards (with a chosen size and a chosen number of patterns). A player could be able to find partial solutions to Eternity II by playing on smaller boards, and could of course do it an infinite number of times via save files. A resolution algorithm was also able to solve boards going up to 100 pieces.

    Public speaking

    Previous Next

    During the first semester of 2017 we were asked to write a 5 minutes advocacy on the subject of our choice. The speech was first presented in front of the class, and if the teacher liked it, was then presented in front of the entire year. My speech along with 12 other pupils was selected to go to the finals, and was presented in june 2017 in front of 300+ pupils and a jury. There were 5 prizes: first, second and third prize by the jury, and two prizes by the public. I won first prize by the jury, and one of the two public prizes.

    My speech was about feminism. It was not about explaining women's conditions today, but rather explaining the history behind the word 'feminism', what it meant before and what it means today. My main point was that women's conditions today in the world as definitely not as perfect as we would want them to be, and that fighting for these causes is still relevant. I also stressed on the point that men too can suffer from inequalities, and that feminism now also needs to cover those aspects as well.
